I spend a lot of time thinking about people and computers, and how the two can best get along.

I'm a user experience designer and full stack developer. I've been in the space since my start at AOL in '98 when I was 16. But really, my start was '92 when I got a 2400 baud modem for Christmas. I started creating things online immediately and I've never stopped.

I believe in humanity and in computers. But our shared potential is often lost, and we call it user experience - the cognitive bottleneck of human-computer interaction. I believe that if we apply empathy to both, we can craft experiences that meet the emotional needs of users and the rational needs of computers, allowing us to achieve amazing things together.
